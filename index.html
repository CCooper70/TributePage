ELVIS PRESLEY

Some interesting things about Elvis Presley

1935: January 8, 1935, Tupelo, MS, Elvis Aaron Presley was born to Vernon and Gladys Presley. He is the surviving twin of brother Jesse Garon Presley.

1946: Elvis received his first guitar from his mother on his 11th birthday. He has his first taste of musical success a few years later, when he won a talent show at Humes High School in Memphis, TN.

1955: Elvis began to develop a following with fans. He signed a record deal with RCARecords, that was worked out by his manager, Colonel Tom Parker.

1956: He got his first #1 single "Heartbreak Hotel", and then signed a movie contract with Paramount Pictures. His first film "Love Me Tender" was a box office hit.

1967: While still in Europe, he met and fell in love with a young teenager, Priscilla Bfeaulieu. They were married on may 1, 1967. One year later they had a daughter, Lisa Marie.

1973: Elvis and Priscilla got divorced and she received full custody of their daughter. Elvis was fighting his addiction to prescription drugs, also batteling with aweight problem.

1977: Elvis performed his last concert, in June. He then returned home to Graceland, to prepare for his next tour. On August 16, 1977 Elvis passed away from heart failure, at the age of 42.

It was later revealed Elvis' heart ailure was related to his prescription addiction. He was laid to rest at his home, Graceland, near his parents and grandmother.

Elvis' passing brought saddness to many. The FTD flower service reported more than 3,100 floral arrangements were ordered the day he died.

On Augus 18, 1977, an estimated 18,000 people, with signs, flowers, and memorabilia, lined Elvis Presley Boulevard in Memphis, TN, to catch a glimpse of the funeral procession. Over 200 family members attended a private ceremony at Graceland. Celebrities and notable figures where in attendence also. Some of the attendees were, john Wayne, Burt Reynolds, Ann-margret, and Caroline Kennedy, to name a few.

Elvis was survived by his only daughter, Lisa Marie (1967-2023), and four grandchildren.
